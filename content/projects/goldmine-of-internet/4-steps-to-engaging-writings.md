# 4 Steps to Engaging Writings

Study the lives and practices of today’s most successful entrepreneurs, financiers, and builders and you’ll find one common trait: a deep, visceral understanding of the importance of powerful, efficient, high-leverage writing.

Why? These leaders know that powerful writing isn't an accident—clear writing is clear thinking.

The four key principles of powerful business writing are:
1. Draft Fast, Edit Slow
2. KISS (Keep It Simple, Stupid)
3. Clear Target Reaction
4. Storytelling

## Principle 1: Draft Fast, Edit Slow

There's nothing more daunting than a blank page. The solution? Start writing, fast. Get a draft down—and don’t worry at all about the quality. As Julian Shapiro says, “Making something bad then iterating until it’s good is faster than making something good upfront.”

A helpful framework is **Write-Rest-Review**:
1. Get the first draft down as quickly as possible.
2. Walk away for 5 minutes.
3. Come back and review the draft with fresh eyes. Ask questions like: What's missing? Where are the logical flaws? Where is the writing loose?

## Principle 2: Keep It Simple, Stupid

Great business writing is simple and direct. There's no fluff.

### Cut the Fluff
In school, we were taught to add descriptors. In the real world, those fluff words are the silent killers of powerful business writing. Be ruthless in identifying and eliminating unnecessary words, such as:
- "I think X"
- "Very" (and similar adverbs)
- Acronyms and jargon
- Overly complex words

### Shorten Everything
Powerful writing is punchy. Use short sentence structures and space out paragraphs to make the writing more visually appealing.

### Add Data
Replace fluff words with data.
- **Before:** "The *majority* of viewers *loved* the show."
- **After:** "*95%* of viewers rated the show with *5-stars*."

## Principle 3: Clear Target Reaction

Great business writing has a clear purpose. What reaction, value, or takeaway should the audience have? Amazon calls it the "so what?" test. If the takeaway isn't loud and clear, go back to the drawing board.

## Principle 4: Storytelling

Humans are wired for stories. Adding a story, even a small anecdote, to a dry topic can bring the entire piece to life. Simple, crisp stories enhance the power of the message.

## Summary

Better business writing is a skill that can be learned. With focused study and practice, you can improve your craft.

To summarize, the key principles are:
1. Draft Fast, Edit Slow
2. KISS
3. Clear Target Reaction
4. Storytelling
